Morocco's Phosphate Value Chain

  • Morocco holds about 70% of the world's phosphate reserves.
  • Phosphates are essential for agriculture (fertilizers) and used in food, detergents, and industry.
  • This map shows where Morocco extracts and processes phosphates.
  • It shows which countries Morocco exports to.
  • It highlights higher-value phosphate products still imported.
  • The goal is to reveal where value is created in Morocco, where it leaves the national production chain, and where it is captured elsewhere through further transformation.
Trade values come from World Bank WITS (UN Comtrade) partner tables using the latest available reporting years (2024 for phosphoric acid and fertilizers, 2023 for phosphate rock and specialty imports). Values originally reported in thousands of USD have been converted to USD for visualization. Sources: USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries 2025; African Development Bank phosphate factsheet (2021)
